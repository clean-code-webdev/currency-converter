    According to the "Investopedia", at least three factore influence the dollar's price: supply and demand, sentiment and market psychology and technical factors.

    Supply Vs. Demand

    Simply put, the more people want something the higher it's value increases.

    On the other hand, the less people want something the lower it's value is. For example, one person wants to pay 1 dollar for an apple. If 2 people now are willing to buy an apple but there's only one apple available, this factor will automatically give the apple seller the range to ask for more money. If now a thousand people want to buy that same apple, the apple seller will now be able to make even more money due to the even higher value the apple will have. On the other hand, if there are a hundred apple and only one person willing to buy only one apple, the apple seller will have to lower their price, otherwise he'll lose them. That's what supply vs demand is about in very simple words.

    In the case of dollar, when the U.S. exports products or services, it creates a demand for dollars because customers need to pay for goods and services in dollars. That will make the price of the dollar rise. The same will happen when people or corporations are willing to by dollars. The opposite is true.

    Sentiment and Market Psychology

    In the case that the U.S. economy weakens and consumption slows due to increasing unemployment or a major crises, the possibility of selling dollars will become real and that will influence the dollar value. If investors for any reason feel like they have better opportunities buying other currencies than the dollar that will make the price of the dollar drop.

    Technical Factors

    In order to trying to predict the dollar price investors will follow news, goverment releases, statistics, GDP, major events, suply vs demand, unemployment and seasonal factors, among others.
